Design the “Delivery Ads & Storefront” page for an AI-agent-powered restaurant growth platform.

Important product logic:
This is not a traditional ad manager.
The restaurant owner should not need to manually operate campaigns, adjust bids, configure targeting, or manage complex delivery settings.

This platform is fully AI-agent-driven.
The AI handles the operational work automatically.

The only main control the restaurant owner should have on this page is:
- setting the monthly maximum budget limit

The AI should:
- recommend the budget
- allocate spend across Uber Eats and DoorDash
- optimize promotions and storefront activity
- explain expected impact
- show performance changes over time
- guide the owner to adjust the monthly budget limit when needed

Page goal:
Create a simple, high-trust dashboard page where the restaurant owner can:
1. understand current delivery performance
2. set or adjust the monthly maximum spend limit
3. see AI-recommended budget levels
4. understand expected outcomes from changing the budget
5. review recent promotion strategies created by the AI

The page should feel:
- simple
- intelligent
- AI-managed
- operator-friendly
- not like a complicated ad platform

Page title:
Delivery Ads & Storefront

Page subtitle:
AI manages your delivery growth automatically. You only control the monthly budget limit.

Main page structure:

1. Budget Control Panel
This is the most important module on the page.

Purpose:
Let the restaurant owner set the monthly maximum budget cap, while clearly showing the AI-recommended amount.

Include:
- Current monthly budget limit
- AI recommended monthly budget
- Difference between current budget and recommended budget
- Simple control to adjust budget limit
- Short explanation of why the AI recommends that number

Example mock data:
- Current Monthly Budget Limit: $2,500
- AI Recommended Budget: $3,200
- Gap: +$700 recommended increase

Example explanation:
Based on recent conversion trends on Uber Eats and DoorDash, increasing the monthly budget limit to $3,200 could help capture more high-intent traffic during lunch and dinner peaks.

Add a simple CTA such as:
- Update Budget Limit
- Use Recommended Budget
- Keep Current Limit

2. Budget Impact Forecast
Purpose:
Show what the owner can expect if they raise or lower the budget.

This should be a simple forecast card or comparison table.

Show 3 scenarios:
- Current budget
- Recommended budget
- Higher growth scenario

Example mock forecast:
Current Budget: $2,500
- Estimated monthly clicks: 4,200
- Estimated orders: 320
- Estimated revenue influenced: $7,100

Recommended Budget: $3,200
- Estimated monthly clicks: 5,300
- Estimated orders: 405
- Estimated revenue influenced: $9,200

Growth Scenario: $4,000
- Estimated monthly clicks: 6,100
- Estimated orders: 470
- Estimated revenue influenced: $10,600

Add a short note:
Forecasts are AI-generated estimates based on recent platform performance, conversion trends, and historical promotion results.

Purpose:
Show simple performance breakdown across Uber Eats and DoorDash.

This section should compare:
- spend
- impressions or visits
- orders
- conversion rate
- revenue impact
- trend vs prior period

Create two platform cards:

Make this section easy to scan.
The goal is not deep ad analysis.
The goal is to reassure the owner that AI is actively managing the channels and generating outcomes.

6. AI Actions & Recommendations
Purpose:
Make the AI feel active, useful, and trustworthy.

This section should summarize what the AI has done recently and what it recommends next.

Example actions:
- Shifted more spend to Uber Eats lunch window based on stronger conversion
- Reduced low-performing late-night spend on DoorDash
- Promoted Family Combo A to increase basket size
- Tested BOGO Milk Tea to improve add-on behavior

Example recommendations:
- Raise monthly budget limit from $2,500 to $3,200 to capture missed peak-hour demand
- Expand combo-based promotions because avg order value is trending up
- Continue beverage-focused offers because add-on conversion is improving

7. Key Takeaways
Purpose:
Give the owner a simple summary without making them read charts.

Example summary block:
- Uber Eats is currently generating the strongest return
- Combo offers are increasing average order value
- Beverage promotions are improving add-on behavior
- Current budget may be limiting growth during peak demand windows

8. Optional small settings area
Because the owner should have very limited controls, keep settings minimal.

Only include:
- Monthly budget limit
- maybe platform enable/disable toggles if needed later
- everything else should remain AI-managed

Do NOT include:
- manual targeting controls
- bid settings
- keyword controls
- audience management
- campaign creation forms
- ad creative setup complexity

UX guidance:
This page should feel much simpler than a traditional ad dashboard.
The owner should feel:
- “AI is handling this for me”
- “I understand what is happening”
- “I can control the budget boundary without dealing with complexity”

Tone:
- practical
- confident
- simple
- AI-first
- commercial
- operator-friendly

Important:
Do not design this like a traditional Ads Manager.
Design it like an AI-managed delivery growth page where budget is the only major owner input, and the rest is transparent reporting plus AI recommendations.
